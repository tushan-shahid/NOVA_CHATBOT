Ralph Waldo Emerson has said “For every minute you remain angry, you give up sixty seconds of peace of mind.”
Alphonso Davies said “Life is too short to be angry or sad for long.”
Lena Horne said “Don’t be afraid to feel angry or as loving as you can, because when you feel nothing it’s just death.”
A good lesson from Grace Kelly would be “Getting angry doesn’t solve anything.”
Arundhati Roy said “If we were to lose the ability to be emotional, if we were to lose the ability to be angry, to be outraged, we would be robots. And I refuse that.”
A great quote from Buddha that may help you is that “Holding on to anger is like grasping a hot coal with the intent of throwing it at someone else; you are the one who gets burned.”
Mark Twain would say “Anger is an acid that can do more harm to the vessel in which it is stored than to anything on which it is poured.”
