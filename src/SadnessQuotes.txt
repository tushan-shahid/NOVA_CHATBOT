Data Von Teese said “Some days are just bad days, that’s all. You have to experience sadness to know happiness, and I remind myself that not every day is going to be a good day, that’s just the way it is!”
Jim Rohn said that “The walls we build around us to keep sadness out also keeps out the joy.” Sharing about your feelings can help break that wall.
Alyssa Milano would say that you should “First, accept sadness. Realize that without losing, winning isn’t so great.”
Taraji P. Henson said that “Every human walks around with a certain kind of sadness. They may not wear it on their sleeves, but it’s there I you look deep.”
Mike Mills said “There’s great sadness and life doesn’t work out like you would want, on a lot of levels, but there’s no need to feel all alone. This happens to everybody, so there’s no self-pity. This is the ride that humans are on, and all of it is essential for our natural part of it.”
Talking can help clear your emotions. As Mandy Ingber said “Each organ is related to an emotion, and the lungs are related to grief. When you clear your lungs, you eliminate grief and sadness.”
