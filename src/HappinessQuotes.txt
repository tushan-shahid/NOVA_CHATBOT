Mahatma Gandhi said that “Happiness is when what you think, what you say, and what you do are in harmony.”
The greek philosopher Epictetus said “There is only one way to happiness and that is to cease worrying about things which are beyond the power of our will.”
John Barrymore said that “Happiness often sneaks in through a door you didn’t know you left open.” Sharing emotions can open many doors.
William Arthur Ward said that “Happiness is an inside job.” It is you that can bring yourself happiness.
Lawrence G. Lovasik says that you should “try to make at least one person happy everyday. If you cannot do a kind deed, speak a kind word. If you cannot speak a kind word, think a kind thought.”
Ernie Banks said that “You must try to generate happiness within yourself. If you aren’t happy in one place, chances are you won’t be happy anyplace.”
